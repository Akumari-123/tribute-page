Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India and People's President"

Why I Admire Him

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was not only a brilliant scientist but also a humble and inspiring leader. His journey from a small town boy to becoming the 11th President of India is a story of determination, hard work, and service to the nation.

His Contributions

Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a key role in India's missile development and nuclear program. Beyond science, he believed in empowering youth and emphasized the importance of education, innovation, and dreaming big. His books, especially *Wings of Fire*, continue to inspire millions.

Major Milestones

1969: Joined ISRO and worked on India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
1980: SLV-III successfully deployed Rohini satellite in orbit.
1998: Played a pivotal role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India.
2015: Passed away while inspiring students with his speech at IIM Shillong.
